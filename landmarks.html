Wonders of the Heart of Eurasia

Charyn Canyon

Carved by time and nature, Charyn Canyon is one of Kazakhstan’s most stunning geological wonders, often compared to the Grand Canyon. With its towering red rock formations and breathtaking landscapes, this natural marvel is perfect for hiking, sightseeing, and capturing mesmerizing photos. Its unique beauty, combined with adventure-filled trails, makes it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and explorers.


  • Offers stunning geological formations with vibrant colors.
  • Popular for hiking, photography, and adventure tours.
  • The Valley of Castles is a must-see, with rock formations resembling ancient fortresses.

Almaty Lake

Nestled in the breathtaking Tian Shan mountains, Big Almaty Lake is a shimmering turquoise reservoir that changes color with the seasons, making it one of Kazakhstan’s most photogenic natural wonders. The lake is a serene retreat for those seeking peaceful nature walks, panoramic mountain views, and fresh alpine air. As a protected area, it remains untouched and pristine, offering visitors a tranquil escape from the bustling city of Almaty.


Things to do:

  • Scenic photography and nature walks
  • Picnic with a view of the lake and surrounding peaks
  • Trekking to nearby mountain trails
  • Wildlife spotting

Bayterek Tower

A striking architectural masterpiece in the heart of Astana (Nur-Sultan), Bayterek Tower symbolizes Kazakhstan’s modern development and cultural heritage. The tower stands 105 meters tall, with an observation deck inside a golden sphere, offering stunning panoramic views of the capital’s futuristic skyline. Its unique design, inspired by Kazakh mythology, and its role as a national symbol make it an essential stop for travelers.


Things to do:

  • Visit the observation deck for city-wide views
  • Take pictures of the tower’s futuristic design
  • Explore the surrounding parks, fountains, and modern buildings

Shymbulak

Located just 30 minutes from Almaty, Shymbulak is Central Asia’s premier ski resort, offering world-class slopes, breathtaking mountain scenery, and a mix of winter and summer activities. Sitting at an elevation of 2,200 meters, the resort provides an exhilarating experience for adventure seekers, whether you're skiing down powdery slopes in winter or hiking up alpine trails in summer.


Things to do:

  • Skiing and snowboarding in the winter
  • Take the scenic cable car ride over the mountains
  • Hiking and mountain biking during the summer months
  • Relax at mountain cafés and lodges with spectacular views

Mangystau

Mangystau, a remote and otherworldly region, is famous for its mystical landscapes, including the Valley of Balls (Torysh), the white chalk cliffs of Bozjyra, and the vast Ustyurt Plateau. This region, often called the "Kazakh Grand Canyon," features some of the most surreal rock formations and untouched desert scenery in the world, making it an adventurer’s paradise.


Things to do:

  • Explore the Valley of Balls with its mysterious spherical rock formations
  • Visit Bozjyra Cliffs, known for their dramatic limestone peaks
  • Off-road driving and camping in the desert
